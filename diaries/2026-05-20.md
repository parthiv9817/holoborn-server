## Morning — diagnosing the gloss as upstream

Started with Vipin's "still glossy" on yesterday's demo. The matte-clamp hypothesis from earlier in the week wasn't enough — needed evidence-grade diagnosis.

Extracted the metalRough texture JPEG from production GLBs across May-7 → today. Decoded with PIL, histogrammed the G channel (glTF convention: G=roughness, 255=matte, 0=mirror-glossy):

| date range | G mean | classification |
|---|---:|---|
| May 9 - May 14 (every run) | 233 - 255 | MATTE |
| **May 15 16:55 (FIRST regressed run)** | **130.7** | **GLOSSY** |
| May 19 - May 20 | 138 - 143 | GLOSSY (consistent) |

**Meshy's retex backend changed between 2026-05-14 18:47 and 2026-05-15 16:55.** Their pipeline started outputting ~45% glossier roughness maps overnight. Same dimensions (2048×2048), same texture slot, pure value drift. Not a bug on our side.

This was the diagnostic move I'm proudest of today — turned subjective "founder says glossy" into measurable upstream regression with a precise calendar boundary.

## Shipping the clamp

Wrote `tools/clamp_roughness.py` — standalone post-hoc tool that parses a GLB, locates the metalRough image's bufferView, decodes the JPEG, clamps G channel to a floor + zeros B channel (kill spurious metallic), re-encodes JPEG, appends to bin chunk, redirects the image's bufferView pointer to the new bytes. Leaves orphaned old bytes (~1MB waste, valid GLB).

Council audit at floor=220 → ChatGPT called it "emergency hammer with skin-death risk." Walked back to **floor=200** ("smart first strike, matte-but-alive zone"). G mean lands at 200 — slightly less matte than May-13's 252 but with plenty of headroom over the regression's 138.

Then baked the same logic into `tools/graft_pbr_materials.py` as `--roughness-floor` (default 200), `--keep-metallic` flag, `--jpeg-quality` arg. Critical design decision: defaults are active (clamp ON by default), so `app/services/generation_pipeline.py` picks up the clamp via existing 3-arg call without any caller change. Future Meshy regressions auto-normalize at the source.

## 6 instructive mistakes from today

### 1. Clamped at floor=220 before checking with council

What I did: built `clamp_roughness.py` with default floor=220 because May-13 baseline G mean was 252 and I anchored on "match the baseline."

What was wrong: ChatGPT called 220 too aggressive — "emergency matte hammer" with skin-death risk. Correct framing: "land in matte-but-alive zone," not "recreate exact baseline value." Lesson: when anchoring on a known-good reference, ask what made it work holistically (lighting+albedo+roughness+motion+composition), not just the single numerical value.

Cost: ~20 min back-and-forth + one staged GLB never tested on Quest.

### 2. Swapped only `test.glb` when user asked to "replace the Y test"

What I did: cp'd today's GLB into test.glb only. User came back saying "saw the old GLB only" — because Stage 3's rigged reveal pulls from `test_rigged.glb` (different controller, different URL).

What was wrong: assumed one URL was THE Y-test URL. Actually three controllers each have their own hardcoded URL fallback. Memory saved: `project_ytest_three_urls.md`.

Cost: One Quest test cycle (test subject's time, ~5 min) wasted seeing the wrong avatar.

### 3. Updated `.env` without restarting uvicorn

What I did: changed `MESHY_PUBLIC_HOST` in .env during the ngrok account swap. Did NOT restart uvicorn.

What was wrong: pydantic-settings caches at startup. Running uvicorn kept stale URL forever. Pipeline sent Meshy a dead URL → 400 → fell back to TRELLIS plastic. Memory saved: `feedback_uvicorn_restart_on_env_change.md`.

Cost: ~10 min diagnostic + one wasted real-pipeline run + bandwidth on the failed Meshy retex.

### 4. Enabled URP post-processing without checking MR passthrough compatibility

What I did: shipped Tweak 2 (m_RenderPostProcessing 0 → 1 on 3 cameras) confidently — wanted the ACES tonemap to activate.

What was wrong: URP post-processing renders to an opaque intermediate buffer. Meta Quest MR passthrough requires alpha preservation so the hardware compositor can show passthrough as background. Enabling post-fx fills the alpha hole with black. Parthiv reported "pure dark, no passthrough" within seconds of testing.

Cost: 1 emergency APK revert + 1 sideload + 1 broken Quest test session. ~15 min recovery.

### 5. Staged 5 V1-V5 material variants that were never tested

What I did: after diagnosing the May-13 baseline used Material_1 aesthetic (no normal map, emissive=baseColor, KHR specular ext), built `modify_material.py` and staged 5 fallback variants (V1 no-normal, V2 normal-scale 0.3, V3 may-13-clone, V4 brighten compromise, V5 rough-factor-half).

What was wrong: the simpler lighting tweak (Tweak 1, directional=0) closed the founder loop before any variant was tested. The variants are now orphan files in `results/avatars/`. Was it actually wrong? Mixed — the research that produced the variants WAS necessary (diagnosing that founder's reference was Material_1 reframed the problem). But the 5 staged files themselves were sunk cost.

Cost: ~30 min building staged GLBs nobody used.

Lesson: when staging variants as a "next test if current doesn't work," try the cheapest current option FIRST before paralleling variants. Sequential, not parallel.

### 6. Researched Meshy text-retex pass as the next big lever before testing the cheapest fix

What I did: when Parthiv proposed "retex the retex with text prompt" as a fix, spent ~20 min researching Meshy API docs (does it support text mode? does it preserve rigging? credit cost? negative_prompt support?). Concluded the architectural risk + double cost + no negative_prompt + still-affected-by-same-backend made it lower priority than directional-light disable.

What was wrong: the answer was reachable without the deep research dive — Tweak 1 (drop directional) was a sed line away and Tweak 2/3 risks were unknown until tested. Should have tested the cheapest local options before researching the most expensive architectural one. Parthiv themselves later confirmed: "tried Meshy text-retex, it's dog shit."

Cost: ~20 min context detour. Not nothing, not catastrophic — but a misallocation.

### Pattern across the 6

Multiple of today's mistakes share a root: **assuming one change is local when it has cross-system effects.** Sed'ing scene file (lighting) without checking MR compat. Swapping a GLB without checking other controllers' URLs. Changing .env without checking what cached the old value. Staging variants before testing cheapest current option.

The fix isn't more caution — caution paralyzes. The fix is: before any change with potential cross-system effect, name what depends on the thing being changed. This diary is that in slow motion. The mistakes happen when the diary isn't being held open at the moment of change.
